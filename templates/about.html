Welcome to Uzbekistan

Explore the heart of Central Asia

Tashkent: The Vibrant Capital

Tashkent, the capital city of Uzbekistan, is a bustling metropolis where modernity meets tradition.

  • Khast Imam Complex: A spiritual center housing the famous Quran of Caliph Uthman.
  • Chorsu Bazaar: A lively market offering a glimpse into local life with fresh produce, spices, and traditional crafts.
  • Amir Timur Square: A central landmark surrounded by important buildings and a statue of the great conqueror.

Samarkand: The Jewel of the Silk Road

Samarkand, one of the oldest inhabited cities in Central Asia, is a treasure trove of historical and architectural wonders.

  • Registan Square: A magnificent ensemble of three madrasahs with stunning Islamic architecture.
  • Shah-i-Zinda: A necropolis with intricately decorated mausoleums.
  • Bibi-Khanym Mosque: Once one of the largest mosques in the Islamic world, it still impresses with its grandeur.

Bukhara: The Living Museum

Bukhara, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a city where history is alive in every corner.

  • The Ark Fortress: A massive fortress that has been a symbol of power for centuries.
  • Poi Kalyan Complex: Featuring the Kalyan Minaret, Kalyan Mosque, and Mir-i-Arab Madrasah.
  • Lyab-i Hauz: A picturesque plaza centered around a pool, surrounded by historic buildings.
